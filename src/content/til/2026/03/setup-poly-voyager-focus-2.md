Today I learned how to set up the Poly Voyager Focus 2 UC headset. It supports multi-device pairing and high-fidelity audio, but requires specific configuration steps for the best experience.

## The Problem

Setting up a professional UC headset like the Voyager Focus 2 can be confusing because it involves multiple connection methods: direct Bluetooth to mobile, connection via a dedicated USB-A/USB-C adapter (BT700) for PC, and pairing with an office base station.

## The Solution

### 1. Entering Pairing Mode
For any new pairing, slide and hold the **Power** switch away from the off position until the LEDs flash **red and blue** and you hear "pairing".

### 2. Pairing with Mobile Devices
Activate Bluetooth on your phone and select **"Poly VFOCUS2 Series"**. The headset can store up to 8 paired devices but maintains only 2 active connections at once.

### 3. Connecting to PC (via BT700 Adapter)
The included Bluetooth USB adapter provides better range and softphone integration than standard built-in Bluetooth.
- Insert the adapter; it should be pre-paired to the headset.
- **Pro Tip**: Install the **Poly Lens Desktop App** to customize headset behavior and update firmware.

### 4. Configuring Audio on macOS
For high-fidelity audio on a Mac:
- Go to **System Preferences > Sound**.
- Under both **Input** and **Output** tabs, select **Poly BT700**.

### 5. Pairing with the Office Base
If you have the Office version:
- Put the headset in pairing mode (red/blue flash).
- Press and hold the **Bluetooth pairing button** on the back of the base for 2 seconds until the display panel icon flashes blue and red.

## Why This Matters

Properly configuring the headset via the BT700 adapter and system sound settings ensures features like "Acoustic Fence" and high-fidelity media streaming work correctly, providing a much better experience than a generic Bluetooth connection.
